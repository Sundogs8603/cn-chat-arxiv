[^7]: 线性二元数据模型与网络溢出中的推断

    Inference in Linear Dyadic Data Models with Network Spillovers. (arXiv:2203.03497v4 [econ.EM] UPDATED)

    [http://arxiv.org/abs/2203.03497](http://arxiv.org/abs/2203.03497)

    在使用二元数据（即按单位对索引的数据）时，研究人员通常假设一个线性模型，使用最小二乘法进行估计，并使用“二元鲁棒”方差估计器进行推断。后者假设如果二元数据不共享一个单位（例如，相同个体不同时出现在两个数据对中），则二元数据是不相关的。我们表明，在许多经验应用中，这种假设并不成立，因为由于网络连接而可能存在间接链路，生成相关的结果。因此，在这种情况下，“二元鲁棒”估计器可能存在偏差。我们通过利用网络统计结果，为这种情境开发了一种一致的方差估计器。我们的估计器在模拟中具有良好的有限样本性质，同时允许溢出效应的衰减。我们通过对欧洲议会中的政治家投票行为的应用来说明我们的观点。

    When using dyadic data (i.e., data indexed by pairs of units), researchers typically assume a linear model, estimate it using Ordinary Least Squares and conduct inference using ``dyadic-robust" variance estimators. We show that this assumption does not hold in many empirical applications because indirect links may exist due to network connections, generating correlated outcomes. Hence, ``dyadic-robust'' estimators can be biased in such situations. We develop a consistent variance estimator for such contexts by leveraging results in network statistics. Our estimator has good finite sample properties in simulations, while allowing for decay in spillover effects.     At What Level Should One Cluster Standard Errors in Paired and Small-Strata Experiments?. (arXiv:1906.00288v10 [econ.EM] UPDATED)

    [http://arxiv.org/abs/1906.00288](http://arxiv.org/abs/1906.00288)

    在配对和小层次实验中，研究人员发现，将标准误差聚类在随机化单元层次上会导致高达16.5%的错误结论。为解决这个问题，他们应该将标准误差聚类在配对层次上。

    

    在配对实验中，研究人员通常通过将结果回归到治疗指示器和配对固定效应，并在随机化单元层次上聚类标准误差来估计治疗效应。我们表明，即使治疗没有效果，基于该回归的5%水平t检验会错误地得出治疗存在效应的结论，错误率可高达16.5%。为解决这个问题，研究人员应该将标准误差聚类在配对层次上。通过模拟，我们发现类似的结果也适用于具有小层次的聚类实验。

    In matched-pairs experiments in which one cluster per pair of clusters is assigned to treatment, to estimate treatment effects, researchers often regress their outcome on a treatment indicator and pair fixed effects, clustering standard errors at the unit-ofrandomization level. We show that even if the treatment has no effect, a 5%-level t-test based on this regression will wrongly conclude that the treatment has an effect up to 16.5% of the time. To fix this problem, researchers should instead cluster standard errors at the pair level. Using simulations, we show that similar results apply to clustered experiments with small strata.
